## Welcome to "The Experiment"

*Ecology 3.0: A New Way of Land Use*

What you're about to experience isn't just a campground, farm, or business park - it's an experiment in how land can serve multiple purposes while respecting nature and building community. We call this approach "Ecology 3.0."

**Traditional zoning** would separate this land into distinct areas: residential, recreational, agricultural, commercial. Instead, we use what permaculture calls "layering" - allowing different uses to coexist and support each other.

**What you'll discover on this walk:**

- **Organic farming practices** that work with natural systems
- **Self-sufficiency** through solar power, water management, and local food
- **Living with nature** rather than against it
- **Community businesses** that support the local economy
- **Recreation** that connects people to the land

This isn't just about camping or farming - it's about creating a model for how communities can thrive while respecting the environment. Every field, trail, and building you'll see tells part of this story.

**As you walk, consider:** How do these different uses support each other? What would it look like if more places worked this way?

**Begin your journey** - Follow the Orchard Trail to explore each element of our experiment

## Sign Goals

- **Vision introduction** - Explain the "Ecology 3.0" concept
- **Educational foundation** - Set up the learning experience for the trail
- **Philosophical framework** - Help visitors understand the deeper purpose
- **Trail motivation** - Encourage thoughtful exploration
- **Community vision** - Connect to broader environmental and social goals
- **Reflection prompt** - Encourage visitors to think about the implications

- Position as the "deep dive" introduction to the experience

- Emphasize the experimental and innovative nature
