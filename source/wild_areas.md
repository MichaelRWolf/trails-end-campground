## Overview

The wild areas of Trails End Campground represent the natural environment that forms the foundation of the Ecology 3.0 vision. These spaces demonstrate how humans can coexist with and enhance natural systems rather than separating from them.

## Natural Features

### First Growth Forest

- Mature forest areas with original tree species
- Habitat for native wildlife
- Carbon sequestration and air purification
- Natural beauty and recreational value

### Wetlands and Water Features

- Natural water filtration systems
- Wildlife habitat and migration corridors
- Flood control and water storage
- Educational opportunities about water cycles

### Wildflower Meadows

- Native plant communities
- Pollinator habitat and food sources
- Biodiversity conservation
- Seasonal beauty and color

### Wildlife Corridors

- Natural pathways for animal movement
- Connection between habitat areas
- Integration with human trail systems
- Educational opportunities about wildlife

## Ecological Principles

### Aaron's Ecology Framework

Aaron has developed a framework for understanding human-nature relationships:

#### Ecology 1.0

Humans begin to study nature, especially how humans relate to and change nature.

#### Ecology 2.0

In an effort to prevent human damage to nature, we separate from it. Create no-build zones, development zones, urban refuges where humans are not allowed.

By balancing nature versus humans, there is an implicit separation of the two, and an implied zero-sum game where gains for one are losses for another.

This separation spawned "Nature Deficit Disorder," indicating how much our millennia of evolution made us depend on nature for our needs - physical, physiological, spiritual, emotional, mental health.

#### Ecology 3.0

Building on previous steps, we integrate with and co-exist with nature instead of separating from it. By living in harmony with nature, we do not need to separate from it. There is not the separation from Ecology 2.0. It is not a zero-sum game. There can be benefits to both nature and humans by occupying the same space.

The wild areas are intentionally integrated with:

- **Recreational Areas**: Natural beauty and wildlife viewing opportunities

## Values

The wild areas embody the vision of:

- **Connection**: Direct experience with natural systems
- **Stewardship**: Active care for natural resources
- **Education**: Learning about ecological processes
- **Beauty**: Aesthetic appreciation of natural landscapes
- **Health**: Physical and mental benefits of nature contact
